  • Our Humble Beginnings

    Landworks was established in 2005 by Michelle Dunn (MSc.,BSc). In that time we have designed and installed a wide variety of gardens, from small low-maintenance gardens to multi-thousand pound show gardens for hotels.

  • Wildlife Manifesto

    Landworks is committed to wildlife-friendly gardening. With a background in conservation management, we are well aware of the current threats to our native wildlife and design our gardens accordingly. Peat bogs and the rare species associated with them are vanishing rapidly due to the extraction of peat for the gardening industry, so we only use peat-free compost. We design with water conservation in mind, and use plants which are best suited to the local environment to avoid the need for constant watering and pesticides. We use nectar rich and scented plants as standard (best for bees and butterflies) and stock a range of native wildflowers at our nursery for planting wildflower meadows. These wildflowers were all grown from seed gathered with kind permission from the reserves of the Wildlife Trust.

Courtyard

Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet consectetur.

The central courtyard of these holiday cottages was a bleak, uninviting area that did not reflect the character of this 5-star accommodation. We designed and built a central feature consisting of four L-shaped raised beds to complement the symmetry of the surrounding historical buildings. This central area was paved with original slate flagstones to blend the new garden with the old buildings. We completed the design with a semi-formal planting scheme full of old fashioned flowers such as lavender, lupins, scabious, wallflowers and pinks. The design is low maintenance and incorporates a relaxing seating area.

The clients lived in an attractive period house and had converted the adjoining mill buildings into holiday accommodation, which was due to open that summer. Unfortunately, the area around the new cottage was an untamed wilderness, where an attempt to build a pond had gone very wrong. In order to create the perfect pond, we began by excavating a larger pond with a deep sump to ensure two powerful pumps were permanently submerged. These pumps fed a slate cascade where water flooded into the pond over a staircase of natural slate (our clients were fortunate enough to own an abandoned slate quarry!)
